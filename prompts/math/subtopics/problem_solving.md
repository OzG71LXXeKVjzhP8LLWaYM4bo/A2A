## QUESTION TYPES for Problem Solving (generate {{COUNT}} questions):

Create multi-step word problems requiring logical reasoning and strategic thinking.

### Types to Include:
1. **Multi-Step Calculations** - Problems requiring 3+ operations
2. **Working Backwards** - Given the result, find the starting value
3. **Age Problems** - Relationships between ages now, past, or future
4. **Sharing Problems** - Dividing quantities in given ratios or conditions
5. **Money Problems** - Buying, selling, change, budgeting
6. **Logic Puzzles** - Using clues to find values

### Context Variety:
- Family and age relationships
- Shopping and money
- Travel and distance
- Games and competitions
- Collections and sharing

### Example Scenarios:
- "Aisha is 3 times as old as her brother. In 6 years, she will be twice his age. How old is Aisha now?"
- "I think of a number, multiply by 4, add 7, then divide by 3. The answer is 9. What was my number?"
- "Three friends share $120. Mei gets twice as much as Liam, and Chloe gets $10 more than Mei. How much does each person get?"
- "A shop sells pens at $2 each or 3 for $5. What is the cheapest way to buy 8 pens?"

### IMAGE REQUIREMENTS (when requires_image: true):
For problems with diagrams or layouts:
- **SCENARIO**: Brief description of what's shown
- **ELEMENTS**: Objects, people, or quantities depicted
- **LABELS**: Any numbers or text in the image
- **RELATIONSHIPS**: How elements connect (arrows, lines)

### CRITICAL RULES:
1. All final answers should be positive integers or simple values
2. Age problems: all ages should be positive and reasonable
3. Money problems: use Australian dollars, sensible amounts
4. Avoid problems requiring algebra with variables (use trial/logic instead)
5. Make sure there's enough information to solve the problem
